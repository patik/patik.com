How to handle uncertainty

  1. Create a bare-bones, straightforward structure for your site or app
  2. Mobile first
  3. Progressive enhancement
  4. Stick with the standards

Mobile first

  • Focus on the parts of your site that are really important
  • Why are users here?
  • Decide what's superfluous or extra to the site's usability
  • Navigation? Banners? Shrink them and tuck them away
  • If it doesn't warrant space on your mobile site, do you really need it at all?

Progressive enhancement

  1. Create a solid base with all the core features but no frills
  2. Add non-critical enhancements for browsers that can support them
    • Example: "Type in your ZIP code" for older browsers, but use geolocation when available
  3. Responsive design
    • Every device gets the same HTML, but CSS & JS adjust the experience

Stick with the standards

HTML5, CSS3, supported JavaScript

  • Native app or web app?
    • Depends on your situation
    • Native has its advantages. But...
  • WebAPI, lead by Mozilla
    • Access to device's camera, contacts, files, accelerometer, etc
  • Web-based solutions are more portable
    • Flash, Silverlight, Air, and other proprietary technologies are problematic and inflexible
    • There's the iTunes App Store, the Android market... and 109 others
    • jQuery Mobile
    • PhoneGap
